Registration Options

How Would You Like to Register?

Choose the registration method that works best for you. All routes are legally valid and result in an official NPC-issued certificate.

🖥️
Online

Self Service Registration

Complete your entire registration from anywhere in Nigeria — no office visit required.

  1. 1 Deed Poll drafted and signed by a qualified lawyer
  2. 2 Pay the required registration fees via the NPC portal
  3. 3 Upload scanned Deed Poll, Affidavit & Newspaper Publication
  4. 4 Documents reviewed and approved by an authorised NPC officer
  5. 5 Official certificate delivered to your email address
🏛️
Walk-in

In-Person Registration

Visit any NPC state office to submit your documents physically with the help of our staff.

  1. 1 Prepare all required documents (Deed Poll, Affidavit, Publication)
  2. 2 Submit documents physically at any NPC state office
  3. 3 Documents scanned and entered into the NPC system by staff
  4. 4 Authorised NPC officer verifies and approves the application
  5. 5 Certificate printed and sent to your registered email
✅
Verification

Certificate Verification

Verify the authenticity of any NPC-issued Deed Poll certificate quickly and securely.

  1. 1 Pay the certificate verification fee on the NPC portal
  2. 2 Upload a scanned copy of the Deed Poll certificate
  3. 3 NPC Legal Services cross-checks against the national register
  4. 4 Receive a downloadable verification result report
Documentation

What You'll Need

Ensure all documents are genuine, properly executed, and legible before submitting. Incomplete applications may cause delays in processing.

Submission note: Supporting documents must be scanned at a minimum resolution of 300 DPI and uploaded via the NPC Portal, or submitted physically at any NPC state office nationwide.
Deed Poll Must be drafted and executed by a qualified Nigerian lawyer
Affidavit of Change of Name Sworn before a Commissioner for Oaths or Notary Public
Newspaper Publication Name change published in a newspaper of national circulation
Attestation Certificate (Optional) Issued by a State Government or recognised body
Birth Certificate (Optional) Original birth certificate as a supporting identity document

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

Everything you need to know about registering a Deed Poll through the NPC.

A Deed Poll is a legal document that records a person's decision to abandon their former name and adopt a new one. It is drafted by a qualified lawyer and, once registered with the Nigeria Population Commission, is officially recognised for updating identity documents such as passports, driving licences, and bank records.

Any Nigerian citizen aged 18 or above may apply. For applicants under 18, a parent or legal guardian must initiate the application on their behalf and provide supporting consent documentation.

Yes. The NPC requires that all Deed Poll documents be drafted and signed by a qualified Nigerian lawyer. Self-drafted documents will not be accepted. Your lawyer will also assist with the accompanying Affidavit and can advise on the newspaper publication requirement.

Nigerian law requires that a change of name be published in a newspaper of national or wide circulation. This serves as a public notice of your name change. You must provide a clipping or scan of the published notice as part of your application. Your lawyer can arrange this publication on your behalf.

Processing times vary depending on the volume of applications being handled by NPC Legal Services at the time of submission. Once all documents have been verified and approved, your certificate will be issued and delivered to your registered email address. You may contact the Legal Services Department for a status update on your application.

Registration fees are payable through the NPC Portal. Separate fees apply for virtual registration, in-person registration, and certificate verification. Current fee schedules are published on the NPC website at nationalpopulation.gov.ng.

Yes. An NPC-registered Deed Poll certificate is a legally recognised document in Nigeria. It is accepted by the Nigerian Immigration Service for passport amendments, by banks for account name changes, and by other government agencies and institutions requiring formal evidence of a name change.

If your application is rejected, you will be notified by email with the specific reason(s) for rejection. You may correct the identified issues and resubmit. Common reasons for rejection include illegible scans, missing documents, or discrepancies between the Deed Poll and the Affidavit. Contact the Legal Services Department if you require guidance.
